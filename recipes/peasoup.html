Pea soup

This vegan split pea soup recipe makes a very thick soup.

Depending on the density of split peas,

it may take a while for the vegetables and peas to soften,

but you can't really overcook this soup;

just stir occasionally and add water if it gets too dry.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlix, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 liters water
  • 0.5 liters dried split peas
  • 1.5 deciliters barley
  • 1.5 teaspoons salt
  • 3 potatoes, diced
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1.5 deciliters parsley
  • 1.5 deciliters dried basil
  • 1.5 deciliters dried thyme
  • 1.5 deciliters ground black pepper
  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Saute onion, gaslic, and bay leaf in hor oil until onions are translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add water, peas, barley, and salt; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add potatoes, carrots, celery, parsley, basil, thyme, and pepper. Simmer until peas and vegetables are tender, about one hour.
